Featured Paintings on Canvas

Die Insel (Acrylic on Canvas, 50 x 30 cm)


Die Insel (2024) translated as The Island belongs to the 3-part painting inspired by the charming town of Wasserburg am Inn. This was the last one that was painted to complete the series. The incomplete bridge as seen on the right bottom is connected to the other painting, "die Brücke".


Die Brücke (Acrylic on Canvas, 50 x 30 cm)


Die Brücke (2024) translated as The Bridge belongs to the 3-part painting inspired by the charming town of Wasserburg am Inn. This was the first one that was painted to start the series. The bridge is connected to the other painting, "die Insel" while the fuschia building is continued at the other painting, "am Inn".


Am Inn (Acrylic on Canvas, 50 x 30 cm)


Am Inn (2024) translated as At The Inn belongs to the 3-part painting inspired by the charming town of Wasserburg am Inn. This was the second one that I painted to continue the series. The houses on the left are connected to the other painting, "die Brücke". The bottom right represents the observation deck where one can view the Old Town from across the the river Inn.


Featured Paintings on Paper

The Yurt in Songkol (Acrylic on Paper, 70 x 50 cm)


The Yurt in Songkol (2024) is inspired by my travel to Lake Songkol in Krygyzstan. Some people in the meadows would live in these tents and would travel from one spot to another by simply dismantling and reassembling these yurts. Others would use it to host guest travellers and offer delicious Kyrgyz food and sweets. The wild horses can just run wild in the vast green spaces surrounding the lake.

The Vacation in Venezia (Acrylic on Paper, 70 x 50 cm)


The Vacation in Venezia (2023) is a spur-of-the-moment kind of trip in Italy that I did with the family - which I included in this painting. The colorful buildings in Venice are really impressive and so is the fact that within the Altstadt no cars are allowed, except boats, water taxis and ferries.


The Yak in Tibet (Acrylic on Paper, 70 x 50 cm)


The Yak in Tibet (2023) is inspired by my travel in Tibet. On the way to the Everest Base Camp, one can see a lot of mountain goats and Yaks. But the Yak is a very daunting animal with its black color and horn. It is a source of food and livelihood for the people in the mountain. It is a source of milk and cheese, while its hair is used as blanket or fur.
